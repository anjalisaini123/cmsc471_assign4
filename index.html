What Influences the Length of Atlantic Storms?

The hurricanes and tropical storms of the Atlantic have varying lengths and occur at different times of the year. We wanted to find which factors from our data set impact the length of an Atlantic storm.

Exploration of our data set led us to find that the maximum wind speed of the storm, the diameter of the storm, and the month in which the storm occurred all have some influence over the length of Atlantic storms. The following visulizations will help to show their influence.

This visulization shows the relationship between the wind speed and the length of the storms. (more info will be added later)

The diameter of an area experiencing over 34 knots of wind and the length of the storm has a positive correlation. So, it seems that storms which affect a larger area tend to last longer. Hover over a dot to see which storm it is!

Similarly, the diameter of an area experiencing over 64 knots of wind and the length of the storm also has a generally positive correlation. Note there is one outlier and some storms do not reach over 64 knots of wind speed. Overall, we still see that larger storms tend to last longer.
